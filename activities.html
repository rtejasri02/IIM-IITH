Career Guidance Series

Date: 30th of January 2023, Venue: MSME 110

Shortly after the inauguration of the Indian Institute of Metals (IIM) Student Affiliate Chapter (SAC) at IIT Hyderabad, the IIM SAC team organized a lecture cum interactive session with Dr.Sumedh Gostu (Technical Risk Manager- Hydrometallurgy Expert at M/s. Glencore USA) on 30 Jan 2023 as part of the ‘Career Guidance Series’. About 40 students from different streams including Materials Science and Metallurgical Engineering, E-Waste Resource Engineering & Management, Chemical Engineering, Civil Engineering, and Chemistry have attended and benefitted from this interactive session. He has shared with students the challenges and potential opportunities that are present in the field of metal recycling. He inculcated strongly to the house that “every complex industry problem has a simple solution” based on his experience in solving industry problems. Mr. Vivek, Vice-chairperson of IIM-SAC has coordinated the interactive session.

About Dr. Sumedh Gostu:

Dr. Sumedh Gostu graduated from Worcester Polytechnic Institute with a Ph.D. in 2018 and currently working as a Technical Risk Manager- Hydrometallurgy Expert at M/s. Glencore USA. At Glencore USA, he is involved in the production of Zinc, Copper, and metals recycling. Before this, he was a Hydrometallurgical research scientist and project manager at M/s. Air Liquide. Dr. Sumedh is a recipient of numerous awards in his professional career. Most recently, in the year 2022, he received the ‘American Society of Materials Bronze Medal Award” from ASM Internation for his contributions to the chemical processing of Bauxite Residue and other secondary metallurgical waste materials. He is the recipient of the ‘Emerging Professional Achievement Award (EPAA)-2021’ awarded annually by ASM International to one professional. He has also received the ‘2020 Innovator of the Year' award from M/s. Air Liquide. Dr.

School Visit

As part of an initiative from IIM student chapter IITH to spread awareness and also make young students interested in the field of Material Science and Metallurgical Engineering, the team from the student chapter organized a visit to the nearby DAV school. The visit included a session of one hour duration with the 9th and 10th graders of the school. The session included talks from students who were currently pursuing B-tech Degree in Material Science and Metallurgical Engineering. The talks included topics such metallurgy, use of materials in semiconductors, nanotechnology, biotechnology and other areas of interests too. The interaction from the students were really great and the team was happy to get such a positive response from the students. They were very interested in the topics and had questions and clarification which made the visit interactive and interesting for everyone present. A small quiz was organized and the winners were awarded chocolates as a gift. The visit was then concluded.
